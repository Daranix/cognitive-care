Patient reports experiencing persistent auditory hallucinations described as "whispers" that feel external rather than internal. The voices often discuss the patient or give unclear instructions and are perceived as real despite being unheard by others. They become louder and more distressing at night, leading to significant sleep disturbances, including a sensation of being watched. The voices can be frightening, particularly when they are loud or angry.

Patient also describes visual hallucinations, such as shadows moving and fleeting images of faces, which contribute to difficulty distinguishing reality. Reports a lack of adherence to prescribed medication due to feelings of numbness and disconnection when taking it. States feeling overwhelmed by these experiences but expresses willingness to explore adjusted medication dosages for better management...
